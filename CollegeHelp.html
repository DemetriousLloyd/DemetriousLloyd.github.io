Free Textbooks

LibreTextsTM provides free online textbooks for many subject areas. Unless you are assigned homework from one sepcific textbook, you often only need information on the subject you are learning. Using these resources saves money spent on expensive books and allows for study anywhere with internet access.

Once on the homepage select the dropdown menu and click "Explore the Libraries"

Open Courseware

  • MIT Open Courseware
  • UC Irvine Open Courseware

Many universities have FREE pre-recorded video lectures, PowerPoints, and notes from courses you are currently taking. Finding them is as simple as searching the term "Open Courseware". Doing some searching around these websites may allow you to find alternate versions of courses in case you miss a lecture, have a schedule conflict, or just don't understand your lecturer. Never forget to search for open courseware on other sources like YouTube and Social Media.

Khan Academy has been brought up multiple times. It is a great resource for STEM and non-STEM students alike. A quick search around the website reveals the plethora of courses available. Foreign Language

  • Duolingo
  • Babbel
  • Pimsleur Approach

Having visited over five countries across three continents, I can attest to the importance of learning another language. Sometimes it is a high school requirement, a personal goal, or a temporary necessity. Here are some resources to learn various languages.
Please Note: Pimsleur Approach is quite pricy, but it is very effective for learning how to have a conversation and it has the widest variety of languages I have found. If you cannot find a friend to practice with and are leaving the country for a long period, I highly recommend it. Fair Warning: Each lesson is about 30 minutes, but you have to practice EVERY DAY.

Career and Professional Development

Whether you are seeking a part-time job to pay for college or recently recieved a degree and are looking for work, it is important to network and develop a professional online presence. Below are some links to help you get started.

LinkedIn

  • LinkedIn

Growing up the population's social media website of choice was Myspace, then it became Facebook, this was followed by Twitter. LinkedIn is a social media website for professional development and networking. It can be used to find jobs and connect with people you have worked with. These days LinkedIn has become a necessity in the professional realm. Make a page with a professional photo and take your page very seriously. What you post online stays there forever.

Job Seeking

  • Indeed.com
  • ZipRecruiter

These are websites that help for looking at jobs in your area. Applying to jobs online is necessary and often easier, but it will never beat networking. It is not what you know, it is who you know. Send representatives at your prospective companies an inquiry email or try and schedule an informational interview.

Mobile Banking

Nowadays many people have smartphones. Downloading your bank's mobile app allows you keep track of purchases immediately after you make them. Additionally, you can keep track of your credit card debt to ensure payments are made on time. This can prevent fraud, improve credit scores and assist in creating a money management plan.

Bureau of Labor Statistics

Bureau of Labor Statistics
Researching the job you want is as important. You study for exams, so why not study for your life? The Bureau of Labor Statistics of the United States Department of Labor has on its website, an extensive list of careers held by Americans. It provides data such as average salary and job projections by career. It also narrows down the information by state and county. Search different jobs you might be interested in and play around with the site's data tools.
