Garrett Augustus Morgan Sr: An American Visionary

Here is a timeline of Garrett Morgan's life:

  • 1877 - Born in Claysvill, Kentucky to emancipated parents
  • 1891 - Age 14: Morgan leaves home in search of employment in Cincinatti, Ohio
  • 1912 - Granted a patent for his 1st invention: a fastener for sewing machines (Cleveland, Ohio)
  • 1907-1909 - Morgan opens his own sewing shop, helps found the Cleveland Association of Colored Men, and opens a joint-store with his wife: "Morgan's Cut Rate Ladies Clothing Store."
  • 1912 - patents a smoke hood safety device, later awarded by the International Association of Fire Chiefs
  • 1913 - patents various hair care products: straightening cream, straighteneing comb, and hair coloring
  • 1916 - Morgan uses his new smoke hood, now modified into a gas mask, to save lives after a tunnel explosion under Lake Erie
  • 1920 - helped found the all-Black-member Wakeman Country Club
  • 1923 - his patent for a traffic light is granted and he sells the rights to General Electric
  • 1950s - functionally blind from glaucoma, Morgan invents the self-extinguishing cigarette
  • 1963 - Dies in Cleveland, Ohio at the age of 86
